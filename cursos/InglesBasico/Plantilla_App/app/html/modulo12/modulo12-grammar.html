Grammar Concepts

Al aprender otro idioma también tenemos que comprender las formas gramaticales, principalmente, las conjugaciones verbales. Nosotros, las personas de habla hispana, estamos acostumbrados a usar muchas formas gramaticales en español. Esto no sucede en el inglés, y eso lo vamos a ir revisando en esta sección en todas las unidades.







The use of the past participle 1st part - El uso del pasado participio primera parte


  • 1
  • 2
  • 3
  • Ejemplos

Como recordaras los tiempos verbales en ingles se dividen en tres: presente, pasado y pasado participio. Es hora de estudiar el tercero que en ingles le Ilamamos "Past Participle".
The past participle always goes together with the verb TO HAVEin the present or in the past tense.
El participio pasado siempre se hace acompañar con el verbo "TO HAVE” para las conjugaciones tanto en el presente como en el pasado

Use TO HAVE in the present tense /Se usa TO “HAVE” en la conjugación para el presente:
*When the past action continues into the present/ cuando la acción se inicia en el pasado y continua en el presente:
He has worked for five years; Él ha trabajado por cinco años.
*When the action is stated without any mention of the time of the action / cuando la accion se lleva a cabo sin mencionar el tiempo de la accion:
I have never visited California / Nunca he visitado California.
*When the past action was repeated several times / cuando la action pasada se repite varias veces:
She has felt sick many times / Ella se ha sentido enferma varies veces.

I + HAVE + Worked
¿cómo sería en su forma interrogativa?
Have you felt sick this week?

Y su forma negativa sería:
I haven't (have not) felt sick this week.

Ahora la explicación con varios ejemplos- Las letras en negrita te ayudarán a identificar los verbos, gerundios y auxiliares utilizados, según sea el caso.

Present tense with ING (Presente en gerundio)
Affirmative form Interrogative form Negative form
You are working hard Are you working hard? You are not working hard.
She is buying water Is she buying water? She is not buying water.
They are visiting Paris Are they visiting Paris? They are not visiting Paris.
Future tense (Futuro)
Affirmative form Interrogative form Negative form
You will work hard. Will you work hard? You will not work hard.
She will buy water. Will she buy water? She will not buy water.
They will visit Paris. Will they visit Paris? They will not visit Paris.
Future tense with ING (Futuro en gerundio)
Affirmative form Interrogative form Negative form
You are going to work hard. Are you going to work hard? You are not going to work hard.
She is going to buy water. Is she going to buy water? She is not going to buy water.
They are going to visit Paris. Are they going to visit Paris? They are not going to visit Paris.
Past tense with ING (Pasado en gerundio)
Affirmative form Interrogative form Negative form
You were working hard Were you working hard? You were not working hard.
She was buying water Was she buying water? She was not buying water.
They were visiting Paris Were they visiting Paris? They were not visiting Paris.

Si te fijas, el grado varía. De acuerdo a como fueron utilizados los verbos, el grado más alto de obtener, hacer o desear es Have to, el grado mínimo es Want to.

Diálogo C Conociendo gente en una fiesta

Angela: Oh, hello, John. It’s so nice to see you again. How are you?
John: I’m fine, thanks. Happy birthday, Angela. It’s nice to see you too. How are you?
Angela: I’m fine. Thanks for coming to the party.
John: Angela, this is my friend, Laura.
Angela: Hi, Laura.
Laura: Hello, Angela. Happy Birthday.
Angela: Thank you. It’s nice to meet you, Laura.
Laura: Here is a present for you.
Angela: Oh, thank you. That’s very nice of you. Would you like something to drink?
Laura: Yes. A lemonade please.
Angela: John, would you like something to drink?
John: A Coke please.
Angela: Here you are. Enjoy!
Laura, John: Thanks!
Angela: You’re welcome.
